Parasite Protocols

Cattle and Horse

Copra culture of L3 worms

Reagents required:

  • fresh fecal samples
  • Horticultural vermiculite
  • wooden tongue depressors
  • drinking glasses (7 oz, straight-sided glass with smooth rim)
  • incubator
  • plastic petri-dishes (60mm and 90mm diameter petri dishes)
  • suction pump
  • tap water
  • spray bottle
  • paper towel
  • 1.5 ml screw-top tubes
  • Mol. grade ethanol (95-100%)

Set-up

  1. Label side of drinking glass with date of set-up and sample ID number. Also write the same ID number on the bottom of the glass.
  2. Weigh-out 50 g of faecal sample and place in labelled glass using spatula or wooden tongue depressor.

The quantity of fecal sample per glass can be varied depending on whats available (10-80g) as long as this is noted down and is consistent between samples to be compared

  1. Add approximately equal amounts of vermiculite onto of sample and spray with enough tap water to bind.
  2. Use tongue depressor to mix faecal sample with vermiculite and spray with more water until correct consistency obtained.

It should not be too runny or too dry!

  1. Push mixture down to bottom of glass and make a hole in the middle of mixture with tongue depressor, pressing mixture to sides of glass.
  2. Wipe around top of glass with paper towel to remove fecal material from the rim of the glass.
  3. Roll-up and twist small strips of paper towel and place one over rim of glass. This will allow ventilation when a plastic petri dish (90 mm diameter) is placed over the top of glass.
  4. Place glasses on tray and note down the date they are due to be harvested. This will be 21 days from the day of set-up if placed in incubator at \(25\,^{\circ}{\rm C}\).
  5. Every 3-4 days spray samples with tap water to keep them moist.

Harvest

  1. Add warm tap water to the very top of each glass. Wait for air bubbles to subside.
  2. Place plastic petri dish (90 mm diameter) on top and carefully invert. It is best to avoid big pockets of air.
  3. Add warm water to inverted petri dish and leave for a minimum of 4 hours (or overnight) for L3s to swim out of mixture in glass and into the clean water of the petri-dish.
  4. Use plastic Pasteur pipette to collect water (hopefully containing the L3s!) from the petri dish and place into a smaller petir dish (60 mm diameter) to allow examination under an inverted microscope.
  5. Count or estimate number of L3s present.
  6. Transfer water containing L3s to a 15 ml falcon tube and allow to settle at \(4\,^{\circ}{\rm C}\) for 1 hour.
  7. Set the centrifuge to \(0\,^{\circ}{\rm C}\) and spin L3s at 4500 G for 5 min.
  8. Remove/discard supernatant down to 1.5 ml with suction pump.
  9. Transfer to 1.5 ml screww-top microtube and Spin at 13000 G for 3 min to pellet worms.
  10. Remove supernatent with pipette down to 300 \(\mu\)l.
  11. Add 700 \(\mu\)l of 95-100% mol grade ethanol to fix the L3s.
  12. Label tube and store at \(4\,^{\circ}{\rm C}\) until needed.

Sheep

Culture of L1 worms

Reagents required:

  • 50 ml falcon tubes
  • 1.5 ml screw-top tubes
  • 13% NaCl (w/v, sp gravity 1.06)
  • Beaker
  • Kitchen sieve
  • Wooden tongue depressors
  • Mol Grade ethanol (95-100%)
  • 20 \(\mu\)l mesh testing sieve

Method

  1. Take 24 g of fecal sample, place in beaker and add 100 ml 13% NaCl. Homogenise with tongue depressor.
  2. Pour suspension over coarse kitchen sieve, collect egg-containing flow-through and aliquot to 4 separate 50 ml falcon tubes (so there is 25 ml in each).

If egg counts are low the amount of sample used and the number of tubes can be increased. We have found that we are getting approx. 30-40% of eggs back using this procedure. So with a FEC of 50 epg we use 72 g fecal sample, 300 ml 13% NaCl and 12 tubes to guarantee 1000 eggs.

  1. Centrifuge at 3600 G at room temp for 5 min. The eggs will float. Pour egg-containing supernatant into another 50 ml tube.
  2. Add equal-volume of tap water.
  3. Mix and spin again at same speed for 5 min. The eggs should pellet. Remove/discard supernatant.
  4. Add 25 ml 13% NaCl to each tube to re-suspend pellet. Repeat steps 3-5.
  5. Re-suspend pellet in 25 ml of water per tube and pour through the pre-wetted 20 \(\mu\)m sieve into the sink. Eggs will be trapped in the sieve. Wash the eggs vigorously with water to remove remaining salt solution. (They donâ€™t like salt!).
  6. Flush the eggs to one corner of the 20 \(\mu\)m sieve, and use the wash bottle with tap water to rinse them into a fresh 90 mm diameter petri dish (with lid).

Wash the sieve well between samples to avoid cross-contamination between samples.

  1. Place eggs in incubator for L1s to hatch at \(24\,^{\circ}{\rm C}\) for 24-48 hours. Estimate egg hatch rate by counting the number of eggs and L1s in an aliquot or a field of vision down a microscope.

Check egg hatch-rate after 24 hours but can be left for 48 hours to achieve maximum egg hatch. Top-up water if evaporation is a big problem.

  1. Spin suspension of L1s in 15 ml falcon tube, remove supernatant down to 300 \(\mu\)l. Add 700 \(\mu\)l of 95-100% mol grade ethanol to fix L1s and transfer to 1.5 ml screwtop tube. Write details on tube and store at \(4\,^{\circ}{\rm C}\) until needed.
